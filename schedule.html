How the two modes differ

Twitch's API doesn't expose a stream's real timezone, only its broadcast language. "Time of day" estimates each stream's local clock from a language→region lookup (documented in EDA.py as LANGUAGE_TZ_OFFSET_HOURS) — a deliberate approximation, most useful for spotting whether local evenings simply beat local mornings everywhere. "Standardized" needs no such guess: it's the literal UTC clock, and is what you'd use to target a specific real-world audience window.

Ratios are total viewers divided by distinct concurrent streamers for that day/hour bucket, aggregated across all months in the dataset. Empty cells mean the scraper simply never took a snapshot in that slot.
